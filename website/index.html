Vertex Project

VERTEX focuses on the design of an orchestrated multi-agent system for eVTOL and vertiport operations

Innovation and Research

About Vertex

This international collaborative research project, titled Vertiport Ecosystem for future urban air mobility (VERTEX), we focus on the study of AI agents for eVTOL and vertiport operations because of their central role in ensuring the performance, safety, and resilience of the broader UAM ecosystem. eVTOL operations involve several critical challenges, including autonomous or human-supervised decision-making, route adaptation under uncertainty, battery and charging constraints, fault detection and recovery, and safe operation in dense and highly variable urban environments.

Vertiports, in turn, must satisfy a wide range of demanding operational requirements, including the capacity to accommodate multiple eVTOLs, compliance with noise and environmental regulations, adaptation to changing weather conditions, and the management of limited energy and charging resources. In addition, vertiports operate under a complex regulatory landscape shaped by federal, state, municipal, and environmental authorities. They may also experience high traffic demand during peak periods and remain exposed to operational failures, cascading disruptions, and security threats, including cybersecurity risks.

Failures or inefficiencies in vertiport operations may result in accidents, service interruptions, and substantial economic and societal losses. These challenges highlight the need for intelligent, trustworthy, and robust AI-based solutions capable of improving the reliability, adaptability, and safety assurance of next-generation UAM infrastructure.

Three tightly interdependent pillars

Our approach is structured around tightly interdependent pillars to solve UAM challenges as mentioned

Multi-Agent Architecture

Design and develop a trustworthy multi-agent architecture for the UAM ecosystem, enabling orchestrated and coordinated decision-making among eVTOLs, vertiports and traffic management services. In our proposal, all system components could potentially be represented by AI agents. This architecture will support distributed intelligence, adaptive planning, and cooperative responses to dynamic operational conditions, thereby improving autonomy, scalability, efficiency, and safety in complex UAM scenarios.

Communication Framework

In order to address the need for reliable, secure, and real-time coordination among multiple actors in the UAM ecosystem, we will develop a communication framework that supports interoperable and context-aware information exchange among eVTOLs, vertiports, air traffic services, infrastructure, and human operators. The framework will also consider the use of advanced wireless networks, such as 5G and beyond, to enhance low-latency communication, operational robustness, and resilience against failures and cybersecurity threats. In addition, the communication framework will incorporate LLM-based semantic capabilities to enable agents to interpret and generate actionable information from both structured and unstructured data sources.

Trust Layer

To establish secure and decentralized relationships across the UAM ecosystem, we propose a trust layer based on Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI) and blockchain technologies. Within this layer, all entities, including AI agents, are endowed with SSI, enabling robust cryptographical mechanisms for authentication, authorization, and the exchange of reliable information (using verifiable credentials standard). This approach ensures interoperability and auditability, while fostering trusted collaboration among heterogeneous stakeholders and services
